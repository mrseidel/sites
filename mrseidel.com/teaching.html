Teaching Philosophy

No matter how good teaching may be, each student must take the responsibility for their own education. - John Carolus S.J.

Within the classroom

Within the classroom, giving students freedom of choice, releasing responsibility of learning to the student, and ensuring enhancement of critical thinking and problem solving skills, are at the forefront of my educational pedagogy.

These philosophies are ones that I live by as I continue to learn about different things in my own personal and professional life.

Education is not the filling of a pail, but the lighting of a fire. - William Butler Yeats

By the end of high school

One of the main directions of my classes is for students to because advocates of their own education. By the time students have completed Grade 12, they should have a good idea of who they are, how they think, how they learn and what they are passionate about.

The object of education is to prepare the young to educate themselves throughout their lives. - Robert M. Hutchins

Sharing and Licensing

Collaboration, sharing, and the need to give back to a community is another source of passion of mine, and it is one that I continuously hope to inspire my students, peers, and fellow teachers to follow.

As you peruse through this website, you will notice that the majority of my work has been licensed in such a way that there is a lot of freedom for re-use. This includes the use of Creative Commons Licenses for my written work and Open Source Licenses for my computer programs/code.
